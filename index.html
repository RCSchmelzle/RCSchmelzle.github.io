Ryan Schmelzle

I'm a PhD student in Computer Science at UNC Chapel Hill, advised by Dan Szafir. My research spans computer vision, graphics, and robotics, with a focus on 3D reconstruction, neural rendering, view synthesis, and perception systems for teleoperation.

I currently work in IRON Labs, where I'm building vision-driven perception and teleoperation systems with a Boston Dynamics SPOT quadruped for search-and-rescue scenarios. Previously, I worked in the Visual Computing & Augmented Intelligence Lab on AR glasses and spatial audio, and in the Graphics & Virtual Reality Group under Henry Fuchs on neural telepresence.

Publications
Publication
 Learning View Synthesis for Desktop Telepresence With Few RGBD Cameras
Shengze Wang, Ziheng Wang, Ryan Schmelzle, Liujie Zheng, YoungJoong Kwon, Roni Sengupta, Henry Fuchs
IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics (TVCG), 2025
paper / doi

A high-quality view synthesis method using commodity RGBD cameras for desktop telepresence. Introduces Multi-Layer Point Cloud (MPC) representation with a temporally-aware neural renderer and Spatial Skip Connections for real-time novel view synthesis.
